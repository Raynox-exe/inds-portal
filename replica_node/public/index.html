About INDS

The Institute for Niger Delta Studies (INDS) is a multidisciplinary academic unit and research centre established by the Niger Delta University Act of 2000 (as amended in 2018) as an independent research institution within the Niger Delta University.

INDS is dedicated to the study of the Niger Delta region with a core mandate to stimulate interest in and promote understanding of the peoples and issues of the Niger Delta through research, education, advocacy and capacity building.

The INDS is thus a bridge-building institute, chartered by its enabling law to promote development and advance the rights and general welfare of the Niger Delta and its peoples by facilitating multidimensional learning and communication focused on the region.

Mission & Vision

To enhance understanding of the development challenges of the Niger Delta, in order to contribute to the planning and implementation of appropriate intervention policies and strategies.

Core Mandates

  • Promotion and understanding of, and interest in the Niger Delta.
  • Study, research and teaching of the history, languages and culture of the Niger Delta people.
  • Proper management and conservation of forest and marine resources.
  • Research on environmental pollution and erosion problems.
  • Organization of international conferences, seminars, and workshops.
  • Establishment of museums and libraries for Niger Delta studies.

Interdisciplinary Research

Advancing knowledge across environmental, social, and economic spheres of the Niger Delta.

Policy Advocacy

Engaging stakeholders to translate research findings into actionable regional policies.

Capacity Building

Empowering local researchers and communities through workshops and educational programs.

Academic Programmes

Education

Empowering the next generation of leaders in Conflict, Peace, and Strategic Studies.

General Philosophy

Our postgraduate programs are designed to produce men and women who are technically and professionally equipped to play leadership roles in the private and public sectors, especially in the areas of conflict management, peace building and strategic issues in Nigeria and the world.

Masters of Science (MSc) Programmes

The philosophy of the Masters of Science (MSc) programme is to provide postgraduate training opportunities for B.Sc. holders in Conflict, Peace and Strategic Studies, and other related social sciences disciplines with a minimum of a second class lower degree for productive research/teaching or those seeking for professional career in conflict management, peace-building and strategic activities in private and public sectors in Nigeria or abroad.

Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) Programme

The philosophy of the Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) degree in Conflict, Peace and Strategic Studies is to enable MSC holders in Conflict, Peace and Strategic Studies and other related social sciences disciplines specialize in areas of Conflict, Peace and Strategic Studies with skills required of higher-level manpower for research and teaching or professional career in higher institutions and industries.

Postgraduate Diploma Programme (PGD)

The philosophy of the Postgraduate Diploma programme (PGD) in Conflict, Peace and Strategic Studies (CPSS) is to provide postgraduate training opportunities in Conflict, Peace and Strategic Studies for professionals in related disciplines, and graduates in CPSS who otherwise would not qualify for admission into Masters degree programmes in CPSS and other related courses.
